Capybaras

Capybaras are the largest living rodent in the world. They are native to South America, but there is a small invasive population in Florida. Capybaras are excellent swimmers; in the water, they need to worry about aquatic predators such as caimans and boa constrictors. On land, predators include jaguars and pumas. Capybaras are known for being docile and indifferent toward humans.
